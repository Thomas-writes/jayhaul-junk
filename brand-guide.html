Color Usage

How It Should Feel

Deep Navy

Use for truck wraps, headers, uniforms, invoices, and large brand fields. It should be the most recognizable brand color.

Orange

Use for calls to action, phone numbers, price tags, arrows, safety accents, and service highlights. Keep it energetic, not overwhelming.

Light Gray

Use for website backgrounds, quote forms, service sections, and printed space where the brand needs to breathe.

Typography

Modern Truck-Safe Type

Cleanouts Made Simple

Recommended system: Montserrat for headings and Inter for body copy. It is bold enough for vehicles, clean enough for websites, and flexible across ads, uniforms, invoices, and quote forms.

Primary Heading

Montserrat ExtraBold or Black. Use uppercase for truck graphics and short service headlines.

Alternate Headings

Poppins for a slightly friendlier look. League Spartan for a more squared, signage-heavy feel.

Body Copy

Inter for digital interfaces and print clarity. Open Sans is a dependable fallback.

Voice

Clear, Helpful, Ready

Say

Same-day junk removal. Clear pricing. Friendly local hauling. We lift, load, and clean up.

Avoid

Overly clever slogans, vague promises, cluttered service lists, and language that feels pushy or gimmicky.

Tone

Confident, practical, respectful, and fast. The customer should feel immediate relief.
